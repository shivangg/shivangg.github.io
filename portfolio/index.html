Shivang Tripathi

Developer who loves to integrate technologies to create interesting project. Loves anything that has to do with the web development or the Internet of Things. With proved skills in project management and design, I'm looking for an opportunity to gain insights into the industry scale experiance.

Robotics is the field that holds the answer to the most inevitable questions that are being or will be asked in the future. Ever since childhood, I wondered how the software interacted with the hardware to make it do interesting stuff. I always wanted to programmatically move things and make it do “intelligent things”.

The initial introduction to this field was made to me by the open-source product, Arduino Uno. Then followed the delightful period of integrating various sensors to do various kinds of actuations. Designing and developing these projects made me realize the complexity of the various systems that have developed in nature because of the millions of years of evolution. Most of the mobile organisms are equipped with the sense of vision. Odometry played an important part in the frictionless working of my projects which were as good as the sensors they employed. While collaborating with my team to solve the problem statement given by the ABU Robocon, I realized the importance of odometry in the mobile systems we design.

Realizing the limited scope of inexpensive sensors in the design of my projects, I decided to work on the most important of senses which, in my humble opinion, I found to be the vision sensors. Having a sense of vision greatly reduces the complexity to solve certain user defined problem like pick and place tasks. I decided to be a part of this exciting and promising field to help in its advancement by integrating various technologies to solve a significant problem.

Artificial intelligence will be the future and only through the extensive use of vision algorithms and sensors the robots will be able to truly learn and evolve at a significant pace. Even though OpenCV is still the best open source library but tracking.js will also be a better option to explore because of its ability to run on variety of browsers, making the vision applications truly cross-platform(Also, because I like JavaScript :bowtie: ).

As for actuation, I have grown special interest in the robotics arm technology. Working on my idea of gesture controlled robotic arm, CopyBot, made me realize the classical problem of Inverse kinematics and the subsequent power of Mathematics as a tool to gracefully overcome such problems. While working in Robot Operating System (ROS), I came to know more about the depth of the robotics research and how easier such robotics framework are making it for developers to create interesting applications by leveraging the work that has previously been done.

I want to make a real-world impact by building under the guidance of the great minds in the field of robotics and essentially understanding the recipe to ergonomic design of robotics application products.
